The Frauenkirche Dresden

Wikipedia entry for the Frauenkirche.

This church was completed in 1743. The dome is 96 meters high. It is made of sandstone and weighs 12,000 tonnes.

George Baehr who was the city architect won the commission. The church would be a square building with a dome.

He wanted the Frauenkirche to be 'like a single stone from the ground to its highest point'.

There had always been worries over the dome. However during the seven years war over one hundred cannon balls hit it without causing major damage.

The church's destruction.

One of the largest bombing raids of the second world war happened in Dresden in February 1945.

Conditions were so castastrophic that fire storms swept the area. Fifteen square kilometers was erased. Yet the church survived the bombing.

However the inside had caught fire and turned the building into an oven. The sandstone could not cope with the extreme condtions. The dome fell to the ground.

The ruins lay there for four decades. Dresden lay behind the iron curtain. The government was not interested in rebuilding a church. People prevented the city removing the rubble.

In the 1960's the city council declared the ruins a memorial against war.
